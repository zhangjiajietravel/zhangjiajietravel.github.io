Best Homestays in Zhangjiajie for Long-Term Stays

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, with its towering sandstone pillars, lush forests, and misty valleys, is a dream destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. While most travelers visit for short stays, those looking to immerse themselves in the region’s beauty for weeks or months will find that homestays offer the perfect blend of comfort, affordability, and local charm. Whether you're a digital nomad, an artist seeking inspiration, or simply someone who wants to live amid breathtaking scenery, these homestays provide an ideal base for long-term stays.

Why Choose a Homestay in Zhangjiajie?

Unlike hotels, homestays in Zhangjiajie offer a more personal experience. Hosts often treat guests like family, sharing home-cooked meals, local tips, and cultural insights. For long-term stays, this makes a huge difference—imagine waking up to freshly brewed tea, learning to cook Tujia dishes, or joining a host on a hidden hiking trail.

Additionally, homestays are usually more budget-friendly than hotels, especially for extended stays. Many offer discounts for monthly bookings, making them perfect for remote workers or slow travelers.

Top Homestays for Long-Term Stays

1. Misty Peaks Retreat

Location: Near Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Nestled in a quiet village just a short walk from the park’s entrance, Misty Peaks Retreat is a favorite among long-term visitors. The wooden-framed rooms offer stunning views of the surrounding karst formations, and the hosts, a local Tujia family, are incredibly welcoming.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Affordable monthly rates (often 30% cheaper than weekly bookings)
- Free guided hikes with the host, who knows all the secret trails
- Home-style meals featuring organic vegetables from their garden

2. Cloud Nest Lodge

Location: Tianzi Mountain Area

For those who want to wake up above the clouds, Cloud Nest Lodge is an unbeatable choice. Perched on a hillside, this homestay offers panoramic views of the sea of peaks. The rooms are cozy, with traditional decor and modern amenities like strong Wi-Fi—ideal for digital nomads.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Peaceful environment away from tourist crowds
- Workspace-friendly with a communal lounge perfect for remote work
- Cultural exchanges with other long-term guests, including artists and photographers

3. Bamboo Grove Homestay

Location: Near Yuanjiajie Scenic Area

If you love tranquility, Bamboo Grove Homestay is surrounded by dense bamboo forests, offering a serene escape. The hosts, a retired couple, love sharing stories about Zhangjiajie’s history and often organize tea-tasting sessions.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Yoga and meditation-friendly atmosphere
- Discounted laundry and cleaning services for extended stays
- Easy access to less-visited trails

4. River Valley Cottage

Location: Along the Golden Whip Stream

This charming cottage sits right beside the crystal-clear Golden Whip Stream, where you can hear the water flowing from your window. The host, an avid photographer, offers free workshops for guests interested in capturing Zhangjiajie’s beauty.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Photography-friendly location with golden-hour lighting
- Fresh river fish dinners prepared by the host
- Close to park shuttles for easy exploration

5. Mountain View Farmstay

Location: Near Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon

For a more rural experience, Mountain View Farmstay lets you live like a local. Guests can help with farming activities, from picking tea leaves to harvesting vegetables. The hearty, farm-to-table meals are a highlight.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Hands-on cultural experience with farming and cooking classes
- Strong sense of community with other long-term guests
- Great for families with spacious outdoor areas

Tips for Booking a Long-Term Homestay

  1. Negotiate Rates – Many hosts offer discounts for stays longer than a month. Don’t hesitate to ask.
  2. Check Wi-Fi Reliability – If you’re working remotely, test the connection before committing.
  3. Ask About Laundry & Kitchen Access – These amenities make long stays much more comfortable.
  4. Look for Social Hosts – A friendly host can make your stay unforgettable with local insights and activities.

Final Thoughts

Zhangjiajie’s homestays offer more than just a place to sleep—they provide a way to truly connect with the region’s natural beauty and culture. Whether you prefer a misty mountain retreat or a riverside cottage, there’s a perfect long-term stay waiting for you.
