How to Use Public Transport in Zhangjiajie

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its towering sandstone pillars, lush forests, and breathtaking landscapes. While many visitors focus on hiking and sightseeing, navigating the city’s public transport system can be a challenge for first-time travelers. Whether you're heading to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, Tianmen Mountain, or the Glass Bridge, understanding local transportation options will save you time and stress.

Understanding Zhangjiajie’s Public Transport System

Zhangjiajie’s public transport consists of buses, taxis, and tourist shuttles. The city is relatively small, but attractions are spread out, so planning your routes in advance is essential.

1. City Buses

The most budget-friendly way to get around Zhangjiajie is by using city buses. They cover major areas, including the airport, train station, and key tourist spots.

Key Bus Routes for Tourists

  • Bus 4 & 5: Connects the city center (near the train station) to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park.
  • Bus 10: Runs from the airport to downtown, passing near popular hotels.
  • Bus 13: Goes to Tianmen Mountain’s cable car station.

Tips for Riding Buses:
- Fares are usually 2 RMB (pay in cash or scan QR codes if available).
- Buses can get crowded during peak hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM).
- Announcements are in Mandarin, so have your destination written in Chinese or use a map app.

2. Tourist Shuttles

For hassle-free travel between major attractions, tourist shuttles are a great option. These are specially designated buses that run between Zhangjiajie’s top sights.

Popular Shuttle Routes

  • Zhangjiajie National Forest Park ↔ Wulingyuan: Frequent departures, takes about 40 minutes.
  • Tianmen Mountain ↔ Glass Bridge: Less frequent, so check schedules in advance.

Advantages:
- Direct routes with minimal stops.
- Comfortable seating (some are air-conditioned).

3. Taxis & Ride-Hailing Apps

Taxis are widely available and convenient, especially for short trips or late-night travel.

Taxi Tips

  • Always insist on using the meter.
  • Have your destination written in Chinese (hotels can help with this).
  • Ride-hailing apps like Didi work well in Zhangjiajie.

Approximate Fares:
- Airport to downtown: 50-70 RMB
- Downtown to Wulingyuan: 80-120 RMB

Getting to Major Attractions

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

This is the main highlight, home to the famous Avatar Hallelujah Mountain.

Transport Options:

  1. Public Bus: Take Bus 4 or 5 from the city center (about 1 hour).
  2. Tourist Shuttle: More comfortable, slightly faster.
  3. Taxi: Best for groups or if you’re short on time.

Pro Tip: Enter through the Wulingyuan Entrance for shorter queues.

Tianmen Mountain

Known for its 999-step staircase and glass walkways, Tianmen Mountain is a must-visit.

How to Get There:

  • Bus 13 from downtown to the cable car station.
  • Taxi: Faster but pricier (around 30-50 RMB).

Note: The cable car ride is part of the experience—don’t miss it!

Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon & Glass Bridge

The world’s longest glass bridge is about 1.5 hours from downtown.

Transport Choices:

  • Tourist Shuttle: The most reliable option.
  • Private Taxi: Costs 150-200 RMB round-trip.

Warning: The bridge is not for the faint-hearted!

Navigating the Airport & Train Station

Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (DYG)

  • Located 5 km from downtown.
  • Bus 10 goes to the city center (cheap but slow).
  • Taxi: Takes 15 minutes (around 30 RMB).

Zhangjiajie Railway Station

  • Connects to major cities like Changsha, Beijing, and Shanghai.
  • Buses 4, 5, and 6 depart from here to key tourist areas.

Insider Advice: If arriving late at night, pre-book a taxi—public transport stops early.

Money-Saving Transport Tips

  1. Buy a Transportation Card: If staying long, a rechargeable card can save time.
  2. Group Taxis: Split costs with other travelers.
  3. Early Morning Travel: Avoid crowds on buses and shuttles.

Final Thoughts

Zhangjiajie’s public transport is efficient once you understand the system. Buses are great for budget travelers, while taxis offer convenience. Tourist shuttles are ideal for seamless sightseeing. With these tips, you’ll spend less time figuring out routes and more time enjoying the stunning scenery!
