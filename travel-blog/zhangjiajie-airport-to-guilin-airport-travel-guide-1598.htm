Zhangjiajie Airport to Guilin Airport: Travel Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling from Zhangjiajie Airport (DYG) to Guilin Airport (KWL) is a journey that takes you through some of China’s most breathtaking landscapes. Whether you're a nature lover, an adventure seeker, or a culture enthusiast, this route offers endless opportunities for exploration. Below, we’ve compiled everything you need to know to make this trip seamless and unforgettable.

Best Ways to Travel from Zhangjiajie to Guilin

By Air

The fastest and most convenient option is to take a direct flight. Several airlines operate between Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (DYG) and Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (KWL), with a flight duration of approximately 1.5 hours.

Pros:
- Saves time (ideal for short trips).
- Comfortable and hassle-free.

Cons:
- Limited flight schedules (check in advance).
- May be more expensive during peak seasons.

By Train

For those who prefer scenic routes, taking a high-speed train is a fantastic alternative. While there’s no direct train, you can take a bullet train from Zhangjiajie to Changsha, then transfer to another high-speed train to Guilin.

Pros:
- Stunning countryside views.
- More affordable than flying.

Cons:
- Longer travel time (around 5-6 hours with transfers).
- Requires planning for connections.

By Bus or Private Car

If you’re up for a road trip, renting a car or booking a private transfer allows flexibility. The drive takes about 6-7 hours, passing through picturesque rural areas.

Pros:
- Freedom to stop at hidden gems along the way.
- Great for group travel.

Cons:
- Tiring for solo travelers.
- Road conditions may vary.

Must-Visit Stops Along the Way

1. Fenghuang Ancient Town

If you’re traveling by road or train, consider a detour to Fenghuang (Phoenix Ancient Town). This UNESCO-listed town is famous for its well-preserved architecture, stilt houses, and vibrant nightlife along the Tuo River.

Highlights:
- Walk across the iconic Hong Bridge.
- Explore the former residence of Shen Congwen, a renowned Chinese writer.

2. Longji Rice Terraces

Before reaching Guilin, the Longji Rice Terraces (Dragon’s Backbone) are a must-see. These cascading terraces change colors with the seasons, offering a surreal landscape.

Best Time to Visit:
- Spring (lush greenery).
- Autumn (golden harvest views).

What to Do in Guilin

Explore the Li River

No trip to Guilin is complete without a Li River cruise. The karst mountains and serene waters have inspired poets and painters for centuries.

Top Experiences:
- Bamboo rafting in Yangshuo.
- Visiting Xingping, the village featured on the 20 RMB note.

Discover Reed Flute Cave

This natural limestone cave is illuminated with colorful lights, creating a magical underground world.

Tips:
- Wear comfortable shoes (the path can be slippery).
- Bring a camera for stunning stalactite formations.

Travel Tips for a Smooth Journey

Best Time to Travel

  • Spring (March-May): Mild weather, blooming scenery.
  • Autumn (September-November): Cool temperatures, fewer crowds.

Packing Essentials

  • Lightweight clothing (layers for cooler evenings).
  • Sturdy walking shoes for hikes.
  • Rain gear (Guilin is known for sudden showers).

Local Cuisine to Try

  • Guilin Rice Noodles (Mifen): A breakfast staple.
  • Beer Fish (Yangshuo specialty): Cooked with local beer and spices.

With this guide, your trip from Zhangjiajie to Guilin will be as smooth as it is memorable. Whether you choose to fly, take the train, or embark on a road adventure, the journey itself is part of the magic.
