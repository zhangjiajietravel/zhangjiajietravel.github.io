Zhangjiajie’s Best Travel Vlogs & YouTube Channels

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, often referred to as the "Avatar Mountains" due to its surreal landscape that inspired James Cameron’s blockbuster film, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. With its towering sandstone pillars, lush forests, and breathtaking vistas, it’s no wonder that travel vloggers and YouTubers flock here to capture its magic.

If you’re planning a trip or simply want to experience Zhangjiajie from the comfort of your home, here are some of the best travel vlogs and YouTube channels that showcase the beauty and excitement of this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Top YouTube Channels for Zhangjiajie Travel

1. The Expeditioner

This channel is perfect for those who love immersive storytelling. The Expeditioner’s Zhangjiajie series takes viewers on a cinematic journey through the national park, highlighting hidden trails, local culture, and the best viewpoints. Their drone footage of the Wulingyuan Scenic Area is particularly stunning.

2. Lost LeBlanc

Known for his high-energy travel guides, Lost LeBlanc’s Zhangjiajie vlog is packed with practical tips, from avoiding crowds at Tianzi Mountain to the best times to visit the Glass Bridge at Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon. His charismatic narration makes even the most challenging hikes seem fun.

3. Kara and Nate

This adventurous couple documents their travels in a way that feels personal and relatable. Their Zhangjiajie episode includes a thrilling ride on the Bailong Elevator and a stay in a traditional Tujia village, offering a mix of adrenaline and cultural immersion.

4. Hey Nadine

Nadine’s vibrant personality shines in her Zhangjiajie travel guide, where she covers everything from budget-friendly accommodations to the best local dishes (don’t miss her trying tuán tuán cài, a regional specialty). Her tips on navigating the park’s shuttle system are invaluable.

5. Flying the Nest

Stephen and Jess focus on family-friendly travel, making their Zhangjiajie vlog ideal for those traveling with kids. They highlight accessible trails, kid-safe activities, and the enchanting Yuanjiajie Scenic Area, home to the famous "Hallelujah Mountain."

Must-Watch Zhangjiajie Travel Vlogs

The Ultimate Zhangjiajie Itinerary

This vlog by The Travel Intern breaks down a 4-day itinerary, covering must-see spots like the Golden Whip Stream, Tianmen Mountain’s "Heaven’s Gate," and the thrilling Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge. Their budget breakdown is especially helpful for backpackers.

Off the Beaten Path in Zhangjiajie

Indigo Traveller explores lesser-known areas of the park, avoiding tourist crowds while discovering secluded waterfalls and remote villages. His interactions with local guides provide a deeper understanding of the region’s history.

Zhangjiajie in Winter: A Snowy Paradise

Most visitors come in spring or autumn, but Samuel and Audrey showcase the park’s magical winter transformation. Their footage of snow-covered peaks and misty valleys is nothing short of ethereal.

Pro Tips from Travel Vloggers

Best Time to Visit

Most creators agree that April-May and September-October offer the best weather. However, winter (December-February) provides a unique, crowd-free experience with dramatic fog and snow.

Avoiding Crowds

  • Arrive at sunrise for popular spots like Yuanjiajie.
  • Visit mid-week instead of weekends or holidays.
  • Explore lesser-known trails like Laowuchang.

Essential Gear

  • A sturdy pair of hiking shoes (trails can be slippery).
  • Layers for unpredictable weather.
  • A quality camera or smartphone for those Instagram-worthy shots.

Hidden Gems You Won’t Find in Guidebooks

The Yellow Dragon Cave (Huanglong Dong)

While not as famous as the mountains, this massive limestone cave system is a surreal underground wonder. Drew Binsky’s vlog captures its neon-lit stalactites and boat rides through subterranean rivers.

Fenghuang Ancient Town

Often paired with Zhangjiajie trips, this charming riverside town is a favorite among vloggers like Sorelle Amore, who adore its lantern-lit streets and traditional wooden houses.

Tujia Minority Culture

Channels like Vagabrothers delve into the traditions of the Tujia people, from their stilted houses to folk performances. Don’t miss their segment on xi la (Tujia embroidery).

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re looking for adrenaline-pumping adventures, serene nature walks, or cultural deep dives, these travel vlogs and YouTube channels offer a wealth of inspiration for your Zhangjiajie journey. From cinematic drone shots to practical travel hacks, these creators bring the Avatar Mountains to life in ways that guidebooks simply can’t match.
