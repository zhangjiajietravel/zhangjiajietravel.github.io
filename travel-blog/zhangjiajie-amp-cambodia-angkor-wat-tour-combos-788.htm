Zhangjiajie & Cambodia Angkor Wat Tour Combos

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

For travelers seeking a blend of natural wonders and ancient mystique, combining a trip to Zhangjiajie in China with Cambodia’s Angkor Wat is a match made in heaven. These two destinations offer stark contrasts—towering sandstone pillars shrouded in mist versus sprawling temple ruins reclaimed by jungle—yet together, they create an unforgettable journey through some of Asia’s most iconic landscapes.

Why Combine Zhangjiajie and Angkor Wat?

A Tale of Two Worlds

Zhangjiajie, often called the "Avatar Mountains" for its resemblance to the floating Hallelujah Mountains in the movie, is a surreal paradise of jagged peaks, deep valleys, and lush greenery. Meanwhile, Angkor Wat, the world’s largest religious monument, is a testament to human ingenuity and spiritual devotion, with its intricate carvings and towering spires.

By pairing these destinations, travelers experience both the raw power of nature and the grandeur of ancient civilizations—a perfect balance for those who crave diversity in their adventures.

Seamless Travel Logistics

Many international flights connect through major hubs like Bangkok or Guangzhou, making it convenient to hop from Zhangjiajie to Siem Reap (the gateway to Angkor Wat). Tour operators now offer streamlined itineraries, ensuring smooth transitions between these bucket-list spots.

Zhangjiajie: The Avatar Mountains Come to Life

Must-See Attractions

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is the crown jewel of the region. The park’s quartz-sandstone pillars, some reaching over 1,000 meters, are best explored via the Bailong Elevator (the world’s tallest outdoor lift) or the Glass Bridge at Grand Canyon, not for the faint-hearted!

Tianmen Mountain

Known as "Heaven’s Gate," this mountain features a jaw-dropping 999-step staircase leading to a natural archway. The Tianmen Cave and the cliff-hugging walkway are highlights for thrill-seekers.

Yuanjiajie Scenic Area

Fans of Avatar will recognize the "Pillars of the Sky", which inspired the film’s floating landscapes. The First Bridge Under Heaven, a natural stone bridge, is another surreal sight.

Unique Experiences

  • Hiking the Golden Whip Stream – A serene trail winding through forests and alongside crystal-clear waters.
  • Staying in a Local Tujia Village – Immerse yourself in ethnic culture and traditional stilt-house accommodations.

Angkor Wat: The Heart of the Khmer Empire

Unmissable Temples

Angkor Wat

The iconic centerpiece, best visited at sunrise when the temple’s reflection shimmers in the surrounding moat. The bas-reliefs depicting Hindu epics are a masterpiece of Khmer artistry.

Bayon Temple

Famous for its 216 giant stone faces smiling enigmatically from every angle, this temple is the heart of Angkor Thom.

Ta Prohm

The "Tomb Raider Temple," where massive tree roots strangle ancient ruins, creating a hauntingly beautiful scene straight out of an adventure film.

Beyond the Temples

  • Tonlé Sap Lake – Visit floating villages and witness the unique way of life on Southeast Asia’s largest freshwater lake.
  • Phare Circus – A mesmerizing performance blending theater, music, and acrobatics, showcasing Cambodian talent.

Crafting the Perfect Itinerary

Sample 10-Day Combo Tour

  1. Days 1-4: Zhangjiajie
    • Explore the national park, hike Tianmen Mountain, and experience local culture.
  2. Days 5-6: Travel & Siem Reap Arrival
    • Fly to Siem Reap via a connecting city like Guangzhou or Bangkok.
  3. Days 7-10: Angkor Wat & Beyond
    • Sunrise at Angkor Wat, explore Bayon and Ta Prohm, and take a boat tour on Tonlé Sap.

Pro Tips for Travelers

  • Best Time to Visit – Zhangjiajie is stunning in spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October). Angkor Wat is ideal from November to February when temperatures are cooler.
  • Visa Requirements – Cambodia offers e-visas, while China requires advance planning (check if you need a visa based on your nationality).
  • Packing Essentials – Lightweight clothing for Cambodia, sturdy hiking shoes for Zhangjiajie, and a good camera for both!

Why This Combo is Trending Now

With the resurgence of post-pandemic travel, multi-destination trips are gaining popularity. Zhangjiajie and Angkor Wat represent the perfect mix of adventure, culture, and Instagram-worthy moments. Tour operators are also bundling these destinations with exclusive perks, like private guides or luxury stays, making it easier than ever to experience both in one seamless journey.

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply in search of awe-inspiring sights, the Zhangjiajie & Angkor Wat combo delivers an epic adventure that’s hard to top.
