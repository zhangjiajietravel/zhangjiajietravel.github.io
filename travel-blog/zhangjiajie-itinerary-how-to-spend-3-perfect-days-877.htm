Zhangjiajie Itinerary: How to Spend 3 Perfect Days

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, China’s otherworldly national park, is a dream destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and photographers. Famous for its towering sandstone pillars, misty valleys, and lush forests, this UNESCO World Heritage Site inspired the floating mountains in Avatar. If you’ve got three days to explore, here’s the ultimate itinerary to make the most of your trip.

Day 1: Exploring Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Morning: Arrival & Tianzi Mountain

Start your adventure early by heading straight to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, the crown jewel of the region. Purchase your ticket (valid for four days) and take the shuttle bus to Tianzi Mountain. Ride the cable car up for breathtaking panoramic views of the jagged peaks emerging from the mist.

Don’t miss:
- Helong Park: A tribute to a local revolutionary hero with stunning viewpoints.
- Yubi Peak: A narrow, pencil-like pillar that’s perfect for photos.
- Shenbing Juhui (Soldiers Gathering): A surreal cluster of stone pillars resembling an army formation.

Afternoon: Yuanjiajie & Avatar Hallelujah Mountain

After lunch at a local restaurant (try wild boar hotpot or Tujia-style smoked bacon), take the free shuttle to Yuanjiajie Scenic Area. This is where you’ll find the iconic "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" (formerly called Southern Sky Column), the real-life inspiration for Pandora’s floating peaks.

Highlights:
- First Bridge Under Heaven: A natural rock bridge spanning two cliffs.
- Lost Souls Platform: A vertigo-inducing viewpoint with unobstructed vistas.

Evening: Stay in Wulingyuan

Descend via the Bailong Elevator (the world’s tallest outdoor lift) and head to Wulingyuan Town, the gateway to the park. Check into a cozy guesthouse or boutique hotel. For dinner, sample Sanxiaguo, a spicy three-pot dish unique to the Tujia minority.

Day 2: Adventure in the Grand Canyon & Glass Bridge

Morning: Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon

After breakfast, drive (or take a tour bus) to the Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon, about 1.5 hours from Wulingyuan. This lesser-known gem offers dramatic cliffs, waterfalls, and emerald pools.

Must-do activities:
- Hike the canyon trail (about 2-3 hours) past cascading streams and lush vegetation.
- Boat ride on Baofeng Lake, surrounded by steep karst walls.

Afternoon: The Glass Bridge

The highlight? Walking across the Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge, the world’s longest and highest glass-bottomed bridge. At 430 meters long and 300 meters above the canyon floor, it’s not for the faint-hearted!

Pro tip:
- Visit on a weekday to avoid crowds.
- Wear slip-resistant shoes (they provide shoe covers).

Evening: Fox Fairy Show

Return to Wulingyuan and unwind with the "Charming Xiangxi" cultural show, a dazzling performance blending Tujia folklore, acrobatics, and the legend of the Fox Fairy.

Day 3: Tianmen Mountain & Local Culture

Morning: Tianmen Mountain’s Skywalk

Dedicate your last day to Tianmen Mountain, known as "Heaven’s Gate." Take the world’s longest cable car (7.5 km!) from downtown Zhangjiajie to the summit.

Thrilling experiences:
- Glass Skywalk: A cliff-hugging walkway with sheer drops.
- Tianmen Cave: The 1,300-step "Stairway to Heaven" leads to a natural arch.

Afternoon: Explore Zhangjiajie City

Head back to the city for lunch at Dayongfu, a famous local chain serving Hunan cuisine. Then, stroll through Tujia Folk Customs Park to learn about minority traditions like hand-waving dances and silver jewelry crafting.

Evening: Souvenir Shopping & Departure

Wrap up your trip with a visit to Zhangjiajie Night Market for last-minute souvenirs:
- Handmade Tujia embroidery
- Zhangjiajie chili sauce
- Sandstone carvings

If time allows, enjoy a foot massage (a regional specialty) before heading to the airport or train station.

Bonus Tips for Your Trip

  • Best time to visit: April–October (avoid Golden Week in early October).
  • Transport: Use park shuttles or hire a private driver for flexibility.
  • Altitude sickness: Rare, but stay hydrated.
  • Photography: Sunrise/sunset at Yangjiajie is magical.

Three days in Zhangjiajie will leave you awestruck—whether you’re hiking through fairy-tale landscapes, crossing dizzying bridges, or immersing in local culture. Pack your sense of adventure and camera; this is one trip you’ll never forget.
