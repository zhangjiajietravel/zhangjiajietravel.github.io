Zhangjiajie’s Best Travel Cafés & Co-Working Spaces

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, with its towering sandstone pillars and misty landscapes, is a dream destination for nature lovers. But beyond the breathtaking views of Avatar Mountain and the glass bridges, the city also offers a growing café and co-working scene perfect for digital nomads, remote workers, and travelers who want to relax with a good cup of coffee. Whether you're looking for a quiet spot to work or a cozy café to unwind after a day of hiking, here’s your guide to the best cafés and co-working spaces in Zhangjiajie.

The Rise of Digital Nomad Culture in Zhangjiajie

With more travelers blending work and wanderlust, Zhangjiajie has adapted by offering spaces where visitors can stay connected while surrounded by nature. Free Wi-Fi, strong coffee, and inspiring views make these spots ideal for remote work.

Why Zhangjiajie?

  • Stunning Backdrops: Imagine typing away with a view of the Wulingyuan peaks.
  • Affordable Living: Compared to major cities, Zhangjiajie offers budget-friendly options.
  • Slow-Paced Vibe: A refreshing break from urban hustle.

Top Cafés for Work & Relaxation

1. Cloud & Mist Café

Location: Near Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Nestled close to the park entrance, this café lives up to its name with floor-to-ceiling windows framing the misty mountains. The interior blends modern minimalism with rustic wood accents, creating a serene atmosphere.

Why It’s Great for Work:
- High-speed Wi-Fi
- Plenty of power outlets
- Quiet ambiance in the mornings

Must-Try Drinks:
- Local Wild Honey Latte – A sweet twist on a classic.
- Mountain Herbal Tea – Refreshing and caffeine-free.

2. The Nomad’s Nook

Location: Downtown Zhangjiajie

A favorite among backpackers and remote workers, this café doubles as a casual co-working space. The owners are passionate about creating a community, often hosting meetups for travelers.

Perks:
- Long wooden tables perfect for spreading out laptops.
- Free refills on basic coffee.
- A small library with travel guides.

Food Pick: Their avocado toast is a rare find in Zhangjiajie!

3. Peak View Coffee Lab

Location: Tianmen Mountain Cable Car Station Area

For those who want to combine sightseeing with productivity, this café offers jaw-dropping views of Tianmen Mountain. The glass-walled design ensures you won’t miss the scenery while working.

Work-Friendly Features:
- Noise-canceling headphones available for rent.
- Ergonomic seating.
- Printing and scanning services.

Specialty Drink: The "Sky Walk Mocha" – topped with edible gold dust, inspired by the cliffside walkways.

Best Co-Working Spaces

1. Wuling Hub

Location: Central Zhangjiajie

This dedicated co-working space caters to professionals needing a full office setup. With private booths, meeting rooms, and even a rooftop garden, it’s a productivity haven.

Membership Options:
- Day passes ($10)
- Weekly passes ($45)

Extras:
- Free local SIM cards for international visitors.
- Networking events every Thursday.

2. Green Bamboo Workspace

Location: Near Baofeng Lake

A quieter option surrounded by greenery, this space is ideal for deep work. The minimalist design and natural light make it a calming environment.

Why Choose It?
- Soundproof pods for calls.
- Yoga breaks organized twice a week.
- Freshly brewed tea included in the membership.

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path Cafés

1. The Silent Forest

Location: A secluded spot near Golden Whip Stream

Tucked away from tourist crowds, this café is a sanctuary. The lack of Wi-Fi encourages disconnecting—perfect for journaling or reading.

What to Order: Their black sesame latte is a unique must-try.

2. Old Town Brew

Location: Zhangjiajie’s historic district

A charming spot with vintage décor, serving locally roasted coffee. The slow drip options are worth the wait.

Atmosphere: Live acoustic music on weekends.

Tips for Working Remotely in Zhangjiajie

  • Time Your Visits: Cafés get crowded around lunch; early mornings are best.
  • Bring a Portable Wi-Fi Hotspot: Some mountain areas have spotty connections.
  • Respect the Culture: Keep noise levels low in shared spaces.

Zhangjiajie’s café and co-working scene may not rival Shanghai’s, but its blend of nature and productivity makes it a unique destination for working travelers. Whether you’re sipping coffee with a mountain view or brainstorming in a bamboo-filled workspace, these spots ensure you won’t have to choose between work and adventure.
