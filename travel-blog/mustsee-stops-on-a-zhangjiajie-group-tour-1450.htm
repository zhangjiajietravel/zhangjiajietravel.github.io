Must-See Stops on a Zhangjiajie Group Tour

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in China’s Hunan Province, is a dream destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Famous for its towering sandstone pillars, lush forests, and misty valleys, this region inspired the floating mountains in Avatar. A group tour is one of the best ways to explore Zhangjiajie’s highlights efficiently while enjoying local insights. Here are the must-see stops to include in your itinerary.

The Iconic Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Yuanjiajie Scenic Area

No visit to Zhangjiajie is complete without stepping into the otherworldly landscape of Yuanjiajie. This area is home to the Avatar Hallelujah Mountain, the towering pillar that became the inspiration for Pandora’s floating peaks. The best way to experience it is by taking the Bailong Elevator, the world’s tallest outdoor glass elevator, which offers breathtaking views as it ascends 326 meters in just under two minutes.

Tianzi Mountain

Known as the "Monarch of the Peak Forest," Tianzi Mountain provides panoramic views of the surreal karst formations. Sunrise and sunset here are particularly magical, with mist swirling around the peaks. A cable car ride up the mountain is highly recommended for those who prefer not to hike the steep trails.

Golden Whip Stream

For a more relaxed experience, the Golden Whip Stream trail is perfect. This 7.5-kilometer path winds through dense forests alongside crystal-clear water, with towering cliffs on either side. It’s an excellent spot for photography, especially in the early morning when the light filters through the trees.

The Thrilling Tianmen Mountain

Tianmen Cave (Heaven’s Gate)

Tianmen Mountain is famous for its natural arch, Tianmen Cave, which stands at 131.5 meters tall. Reaching it involves climbing the 999-step "Stairway to Heaven," but the effort is worth it for the awe-inspiring view. Alternatively, the Tianmen Mountain Cableway—one of the longest in the world—offers a scenic ride to the summit.

Glass Skywalk

Adrenaline junkies will love the cliff-hugging glass walkway, which dangles over a sheer drop of 1,400 meters. Walking across the transparent path feels like floating above the clouds, making it a highlight for thrill-seekers.

The Enchanting Baofeng Lake

Nestled among towering cliffs, Baofeng Lake is a serene escape. Visitors can take a boat ride across the emerald-green waters while listening to local singers perform traditional Tujia folk songs. The surrounding waterfalls and lush greenery create a fairy-tale atmosphere.

The Cultural Fenghuang Ancient Town

While not within Zhangjiajie itself, many group tours include a visit to Fenghuang (Phoenix Ancient Town), a beautifully preserved riverside town with Ming and Qing dynasty architecture. Strolling along the Tuojiang River at night, when the lanterns reflect on the water, is an unforgettable experience.

Practical Tips for Your Group Tour

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (April–June) and autumn (September–November) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
  • Footwear: Comfortable hiking shoes are a must for navigating steep trails and stairs.
  • Local Cuisine: Don’t miss Tujia dishes like sanxiaguo (a sizzling pot of meat, vegetables, and tofu) or sour fish soup.
  • Photography Tips: Early mornings are ideal for capturing the misty landscapes without too many tourists.

With its dramatic landscapes and rich culture, Zhangjiajie is a destination that leaves a lasting impression. A well-planned group tour ensures you won’t miss any of its wonders.
