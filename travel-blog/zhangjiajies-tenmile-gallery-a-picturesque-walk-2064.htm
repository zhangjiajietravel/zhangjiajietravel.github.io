Zhangjiajie’s Ten-Mile Gallery: A Picturesque Walk

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of China’s Hunan Province, Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its towering sandstone pillars, lush greenery, and ethereal misty landscapes. Among its many attractions, the Ten-Mile Gallery (十里画廊) stands out as a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a leisurely yet visually stunning experience. This scenic pathway, stretching approximately 5 miles (though its name suggests 10 li, a traditional Chinese unit of distance), offers an immersive journey through nature’s artistry.

Why the Ten-Mile Gallery is a Must-See

The Ten-Mile Gallery is more than just a walking trail—it’s a living painting. Flanked by karst formations that resemble ancient scrolls unfurling, the gallery is a testament to Zhangjiajie’s geological wonders. Unlike the park’s more strenuous hikes, this route is accessible to most visitors, making it ideal for families, photographers, and nature lovers alike.

The Scenic Highlights

  1. The Three Sisters Peaks (三姐妹峰)
    These three distinct peaks rise dramatically from the valley, each with its own unique shape. Local legends say they represent three sisters waiting for their loved ones, adding a layer of cultural intrigue to the natural beauty.

  2. The Sleeping Buddha (睡佛)
    From a certain vantage point, the silhouette of the mountains resembles a reclining Buddha, a serene sight that often leaves visitors in awe.

  3. The Poet’s Pavilion (诗人阁)
    A small rest stop along the trail, this pavilion offers panoramic views of the gallery’s most iconic formations. It’s a perfect spot to pause, reflect, and snap some photos.

How to Explore the Ten-Mile Gallery

On Foot: A Leisurely Stroll

The most popular way to experience the Ten-Mile Gallery is by walking. The paved path is well-maintained, with gentle slopes and plenty of shaded areas. The entire walk takes about 1.5 to 2 hours at a relaxed pace, allowing ample time to admire the scenery.

By Electric Tram: A Convenient Alternative

For those who prefer a quicker or less physically demanding option, an electric tram runs along the route. The tram includes audio commentary (available in multiple languages), offering insights into the geological and cultural significance of the landmarks.

Photography Tips

  • Golden Hours: Early morning or late afternoon light casts a magical glow on the sandstone pillars.
  • Misty Days: Fog enhances the mystical ambiance, creating a dreamlike backdrop.
  • Wildlife Shots: Keep an eye out for macaques and rare bird species that call this area home.

Nearby Attractions to Pair with Your Visit

To make the most of your time in Zhangjiajie, consider combining your Ten-Mile Gallery visit with these nearby highlights:

Yuanjiajie (袁家界)

Famous as the inspiration for Avatar’s Hallelujah Mountains, Yuanjiajie’s floating peaks are a short shuttle ride away. Don’t miss the Avatar Hallelujah Mountain and the thrilling First Bridge Under Heaven.

Tianzi Mountain (天子山)

Known for its sweeping vistas, Tianzi Mountain offers some of the best sunrise views in the park. The Imperial Brush Peak is a standout formation resembling a giant calligraphy brush.

Glass Bridges and Thrill-Seeking Adventures

For adrenaline junkies, the Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge is a short drive away. Walking across the transparent floor, suspended high above the canyon, is an unforgettable experience.

Travel Tips for Visiting the Ten-Mile Gallery

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (April–June) and autumn (September–November) offer pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds.
  • Ticket Info: The Ten-Mile Gallery is included in the general admission ticket to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park.
  • What to Bring: Comfortable shoes, water, sunscreen, and a camera are essentials.
  • Local Cuisine: Try Tujia smoked pork or sticky rice cakes from nearby vendors for a taste of Hunan’s flavors.

Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, the Ten-Mile Gallery promises a memorable adventure through one of China’s most breathtaking landscapes. Its accessibility, combined with its sheer beauty, ensures it remains a cornerstone of any Zhangjiajie itinerary.
