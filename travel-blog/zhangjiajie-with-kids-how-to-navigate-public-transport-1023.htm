Zhangjiajie with Kids: How to Navigate Public Transport

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling to Zhangjiajie with kids is an adventure filled with breathtaking landscapes, towering sandstone pillars, and lush forests. However, navigating public transport in this mountainous region can be a challenge, especially when you have little ones in tow. This guide will help you master the buses, cable cars, and shuttle systems to make your family trip smooth and enjoyable.

Why Zhangjiajie is Perfect for Families

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a dream destination for families who love nature and adventure. The surreal scenery inspired the floating mountains in Avatar, and kids will feel like they’ve stepped into a fantasy world.

Kid-Friendly Attractions

  • Avatar Hallelujah Mountain: A must-see for its otherworldly beauty.
  • Tianzi Mountain: Offers panoramic views and easy cable car rides.
  • Golden Whip Stream: A gentle hike perfect for young explorers.
  • Glass Bridges: For thrill-seeking families (if your kids are brave enough!).

Understanding Zhangjiajie’s Public Transport System

Zhangjiajie’s public transport includes buses, cable cars, and shuttle services. While it’s efficient, it can be confusing for first-time visitors. Here’s how to navigate it with kids.

1. Getting from Zhangjiajie Airport to the City

The airport is about 5 km from downtown. Options include:
- Airport Shuttle Bus: Cheap (~¥5 per person) but may be crowded.
- Taxi: More convenient (~¥30-50). Ensure the driver uses the meter.
- Private Transfer: Pre-booked vans are ideal for families with luggage.

2. Buses in Zhangjiajie City

The city buses are affordable (¥1-2 per ride) but often packed. Key routes:
- Bus No. 4: Connects the train station to downtown.
- Bus No. 10: Goes to the national park entrance.

Tip: Avoid rush hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM) when buses are overcrowded.

3. Shuttle Buses Inside Zhangjiajie National Park

Once inside the park, free shuttle buses operate between major sights:
- Yellow Shuttles: Connect the main gates to scenic spots.
- Green Shuttles: Run between smaller attractions.

Pro Tip: Board early to secure seats, especially with strollers.

4. Cable Cars and Bailong Elevator

  • Tianzi Mountain Cable Car: A 10-minute ride with stunning views (~¥72 one-way).
  • Bailong Elevator: The world’s tallest outdoor lift (~¥72 per ride).

Kid Hack: Buy tickets in advance to skip long queues.

Tips for Stress-Free Travel with Kids

Pack Smart

  • Bring snacks, water, and a lightweight stroller (for toddlers).
  • Carry a portable charger—kids drain phone batteries fast!

Avoid Peak Times

Visit popular spots like Yuanjiajie (Avatar Mountain) early morning or late afternoon to dodge crowds.

Use Ride-Hailing Apps

Didi (China’s Uber) is reliable for short trips if buses feel overwhelming.

Final Thoughts

Zhangjiajie is a magical destination for families, and with these transport tips, you’ll spend less time stressing and more time enjoying the wonders of this natural paradise. Happy travels!
