How to Spend 3 Perfect Days in Zhangjiajie

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Welcome to Zhangjiajie, a place that feels like stepping into a dream. Located in the Hunan Province of China, this region is famous for its towering sandstone pillars, deep ravines, and lush forests that inspired the floating Hallelujah Mountains in the movie Avatar. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a nature lover, or a photography enthusiast, Zhangjiajie offers an unforgettable experience. This itinerary is designed to help you make the most of three perfect days in this magical destination, blending iconic sights with local culture and hidden gems.

Day 1: Exploring the Heart of Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Start your journey early to beat the crowds and embrace the misty morning views that make Zhangjiajie so enchanting. The Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the crown jewel of the area, known for its unique quartzite sandstone formations and biodiversity.

Morning: Yuanjiajie Scenic Area

Head straight to the Yuanjiajie Scenic Area, often referred to as the "Avatar Mountains" due to its resemblance to the fictional world of Pandora. Take the Bailong Elevator, an impressive glass elevator built into the cliffside, which offers a thrilling ascent with panoramic views. At the top, walk along the platforms to see iconic formations like the "First Bridge Under Heaven" and the "South Sky Pillar" (renamed "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" for tourism). The mist swirling around the peaks creates a surreal atmosphere, perfect for photography.

Afternoon: Golden Whip Stream

After descending, enjoy a leisurely hike along the Golden Whip Stream, a tranquil path flanked by towering peaks and clear waters. This 7.5-kilometer trail is relatively flat and shaded, making it ideal for a relaxing afternoon. Keep an eye out for wildlife like macaques and rare birds. The stream is named after a rock formation that resembles a golden whip, and along the way, you'll hear local legends about the area—ask your guide about the love story of the "Scholar and the Fairy."

Evening: Zhangjiajie City and Local Cuisine

Return to Zhangjiajie City and unwind with a taste of local Hunan cuisine. Try dishes like suancai yu (spicy fish with pickled vegetables) or xiangla fen (spicy rice noodles). For a cultural twist, visit a Tujia ethnic minority restaurant where you might catch a traditional performance. The Tujia people are known for their hand-waving dance and unique architecture. After dinner, take a stroll through the city's night market to pick up souvenirs like local tea or handicrafts.

Day 2: Tianmen Mountain and Cultural Immersion

On your second day, shift gears to Tianmen Mountain, another iconic spot famous for its natural wonders and adrenaline-pumping attractions. This mountain is often called "Heaven's Gate Mountain" and is a symbol of Zhangjiajie's dramatic landscape.

Morning: Tianmen Mountain Cable Car and Glass Skywalk

Take the Tianmen Mountain Cableway, one of the longest cable cars in the world, which offers breathtaking views of the 99-bend road below. Upon reaching the summit, walk the Glass Skywalk—a transparent path clinging to the cliff edge—for a heart-pounding experience. Don't miss the Tianmen Cave, a natural arch formation that looks like a gateway to heaven. If you're lucky, you might see clouds drifting through the cave, creating a mystical scene.

Afternoon: Tianmen Temple and Hiking Trails

Visit the Tianmen Temple, a Buddhist shrine nestled on the mountain, and learn about its history dating back centuries. Then, explore the hiking trails that circle the summit, offering different perspectives of the surrounding peaks and valleys. For adventure enthusiasts, there are options like paragliding or zip-lining, but if you prefer a relaxed pace, simply enjoy the scenic viewpoints and fresh air.

Evening: Enjoy a Cultural Show

Head back to the city and consider attending the Charming Xiangxi show, a large-scale performance that showcases the culture of the Tujia and Miao ethnic groups through music, dance, and storytelling. It's a vibrant way to immerse yourself in local traditions. For dinner, try smoked bacon or stinky tofu from a street vendor—a bold flavor that's a staple in Hunan.

Day 3: Baofeng Lake and Grand Canyon Glass Bridge

On your final day, mix serenity with thrill by visiting Baofeng Lake and the Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon, two spots that highlight the region's diverse attractions.

Morning: Baofeng Lake

Drive to Baofeng Lake, a serene emerald-green lake surrounded by steep cliffs. Take a boat ride across the calm waters while listening to local guides sing folk songs—the acoustics here are amazing due to the natural amphitheater effect. The lake is part of a protected area, and its peacefulness provides a perfect contrast to the previous days' adventures.

Afternoon: Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge

Next, visit the Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon, home to the world's longest and highest glass-bottom bridge. Walking across this 430-meter-long bridge is not for the faint-hearted—it suspends over a 300-meter drop, offering dizzying views of the canyon below. For added fun, try the bungee jumping or zip-lining activities available here. The canyon itself has lush vegetation and waterfalls, so take time to hike the trails if you have energy left.

Evening: Relax and Reflect

As your trip winds down, enjoy a quiet evening reflecting on your experiences. Perhaps indulge in a foot massage or traditional Chinese therapy to soothe those tired muscles. For a final meal, savor some hot pot with local spices, sharing stories of your favorite moments—whether it was the awe-inspiring peaks or the cultural encounters.

Practical Tips for Your Zhangjiajie Trip

To make your three days seamless, keep these tips in mind. The best time to visit is spring (April-May) or autumn (September-October) when the weather is mild and crowds are thinner. Summer can be crowded and rainy, while winter offers snowy views but some trails might be closed. Book accommodations in advance, ranging from luxury hotels in the city to guesthouses near the park. Use public transport or hire a guide for ease—many sites are spread out. Don't forget comfortable hiking shoes, a rain jacket, and a camera to capture the magic. Lastly, respect the environment by following park rules and avoiding plastic waste.

Why Zhangjiajie Should Be on Your Bucket List

Zhangjiajie isn't just a destination; it's an experience that blends natural wonder with cultural richness. From the iconic pillars that touch the sky to the warm hospitality of the Tujia people, every moment here feels like a discovery. Whether you're walking on glass bridges or drifting on tranquil lakes, this place leaves you with memories that last a lifetime. So pack your bags, embrace the adventure, and get ready to explore one of China's most breathtaking landscapes.
