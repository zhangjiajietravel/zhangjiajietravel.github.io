Zhangjiajie Hostels with On-Site Restaurants & Bars

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of China’s Hunan province, Zhangjiajie is a dream destination for adventurers, nature lovers, and backpackers alike. Famous for its towering sandstone pillars, lush forests, and the inspiration behind Avatar’s floating mountains, this UNESCO World Heritage Site attracts millions of visitors every year. But beyond the breathtaking landscapes, Zhangjiajie also offers a vibrant hostel scene—complete with on-site restaurants and bars that make socializing and refueling after a long day of hiking effortless.

Whether you’re a solo traveler looking to meet fellow explorers or a budget-conscious tourist who wants convenience without sacrificing comfort, these hostels provide the perfect blend of affordability, atmosphere, and amenities.

Why Stay in a Hostel with On-Site Dining & Drinks?

Convenience After a Long Day of Exploring

Zhangjiajie’s national parks and scenic areas demand hours of walking, climbing, and sightseeing. The last thing you want after descending from Tianzi Mountain or navigating the Glass Bridge is to wander around searching for food. Hostels with built-in restaurants and bars eliminate this hassle—just stumble back to your accommodation and enjoy a hot meal or a cold beer without stepping outside.

Social Vibes & Traveler Connections

Hostels are known for their communal atmosphere, and having an on-site bar or restaurant amplifies this. Shared meals, happy hours, and late-night conversations over local craft beers create instant friendships. Many hostels also organize group dinners or pub crawls, making it easy to bond with other travelers.

Authentic Local & International Flavors

While street food and local restaurants are a must-try, hostel eateries often strike a balance between familiar comfort food (think burgers, pasta, and pancakes) and regional specialties like Tujia smoked pork or stinky tofu. Some even offer cooking classes where you can learn to make Hunan dishes yourself.

Top Zhangjiajie Hostels with On-Site Restaurants & Bars

1. The Hiker’s Inn

Location: Just a 10-minute walk from Zhangjiajie National Forest Park entrance.

This cozy hostel is a favorite among backpackers for its rustic charm and lively common area. The on-site "Peak Bites Café" serves hearty breakfasts (Western and Chinese options), fresh coffee, and craft beers in the evening. Their "Mountain Burger"—loaded with local spiced beef—is legendary among guests.

Why Stay Here?
- Free guided hikes to less-crowded trails.
- Nightly bonfires with live acoustic music.
- A well-stocked bar with local baijiu and imported spirits.

2. Wulingyuan Backpackers’ Lodge

Location: Wulingyuan District, close to the Bailong Elevator.

A modern yet laid-back hostel with a rooftop bar offering panoramic views of the karst peaks. Their "Cloud Nine Restaurant" specializes in fusion cuisine—think spicy cumin lamb pizza and stir-fried wild mushrooms with truffle oil.

Why Stay Here?
- Rooftop happy hour with 2-for-1 cocktails at sunset.
- Vegetarian and vegan-friendly menu.
- Weekly BBQ nights with live DJ sets.

3. Avatar Hostel & Bar

Location: Near Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon.

Inspired by the Avatar movie, this quirky hostel features neon-lit interiors and a sci-fi-themed bar. The "Pandora Grill" serves creative dishes like "Floating Mountain Dumplings" (steamed buns shaped like the park’s peaks).

Why Stay Here?
- Themed parties (glow-in-the-dark nights, costume contests).
- Movie screenings of Avatar with free popcorn.
- A "secret" cocktail menu featuring bioluminescent drinks.

Budget-Friendly Eats & Drinks You Can’t Miss

Must-Try Dishes at Hostel Restaurants

  • Tujia Smoked Pork Ribs – A Hunan specialty, best paired with a local beer.
  • Chili Oil Wontons – Spicy, savory, and addictive.
  • Sweet Osmanthus Cake – A fragrant dessert made with local honey.

Signature Drinks to Sip On

  • "Jade Peak Mojito" – A refreshing mix of mint, lime, and baijiu.
  • "Misty Mountain IPA" – Brewed by a nearby Zhangjiajie microbrewery.
  • "Fire Dragon Shot" – For brave souls (hint: it involves chili-infused liquor).

Tips for Choosing the Right Hostel

  1. Check Proximity to Attractions – Some hostels offer free shuttles to park entrances.
  2. Read Reviews About the Food – A great bar won’t make up for terrible meals.
  3. Look for Social Events – Pub quizzes, karaoke, and group hikes enhance the experience.

Zhangjiajie’s hostels aren’t just a place to sleep—they’re hubs of adventure, flavor, and camaraderie. Whether you’re sipping a cocktail under the stars or swapping travel stories over a steaming bowl of noodles, these spots ensure your trip is as memorable as the landscapes themselves.
