How to Travel Zhangjiajie by Bus: A Complete Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, often referred to as the "Avatar Mountains" due to its surreal landscape that inspired the famous movie, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. While many travelers opt for private tours or taxis, exploring Zhangjiajie by bus is not only budget-friendly but also an authentic way to experience the region. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about navigating Zhangjiajie’s bus system, from routes and schedules to insider tips.

Why Choose the Bus in Zhangjiajie?

Traveling by bus in Zhangjiajie offers several advantages:

  • Affordability: Bus fares are significantly cheaper than private transfers.
  • Local Experience: You’ll get a glimpse of everyday life in this stunning region.
  • Eco-Friendly: Reducing carbon footprint by using public transport.
  • Flexibility: Buses cover most major attractions, including Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, Tianmen Mountain, and nearby villages.

Understanding Zhangjiajie’s Bus System

Zhangjiajie’s bus network is well-developed, connecting the city center (Zhangjiajie Downtown) with key tourist spots. Here’s a breakdown of the most useful routes:

1. City Buses (Zhangjiajie Downtown)

These buses operate within the urban area and are ideal for short-distance travel.

  • Bus 1: Connects the railway station to the city center.
  • Bus 4: Links the airport to downtown.
  • Bus 10: Runs through major shopping and dining districts.

Fare: Typically 2 RMB (about $0.30) per ride.

2. Intercity Buses (Zhangjiajie to Wulingyuan)

Wulingyuan is the gateway to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park. The bus ride takes about 40 minutes.

  • Departure Point: Zhangjiajie Central Bus Station.
  • Frequency: Every 10-15 minutes from 6:00 AM to 7:30 PM.
  • Fare: Around 15 RMB ($2).

3. Scenic Area Shuttle Buses

Once inside Zhangjiajie National Forest Park or Tianmen Mountain, free shuttle buses operate between major viewpoints.

  • Routes: Cover popular spots like Yuanjiajie, Tianzi Mountain, and Golden Whip Stream.
  • Frequency: Every 10-20 minutes.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taking the Bus

Step 1: Arriving in Zhangjiajie

Most travelers arrive via:
- Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (DYG): Take Bus 4 to downtown.
- Zhangjiajie Railway Station: Hop on Bus 1 or a taxi to your hotel.

Step 2: Getting to Wulingyuan

From Zhangjiajie Central Bus Station, board the intercity bus to Wulingyuan. The drop-off point is near the park entrance.

Pro Tip: Buy your Zhangjiajie National Forest Park ticket (valid for 4 days) at the station to save time.

Step 3: Navigating the Park by Shuttle Bus

Inside the park, shuttle buses are color-coded:
- Green Line: Connects the main gate to Yuanjiajie.
- Red Line: Heads to Tianzi Mountain.

Insider Advice: Arrive early (before 8 AM) to avoid crowds.

Must-Visit Attractions Accessible by Bus

1. Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Home to the iconic "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain," this UNESCO site is best explored via the park’s shuttle system.

2. Tianmen Mountain

Take the city bus to Tianmen Mountain Cable Car Station. The "Heaven’s Gate" and glass walkway are highlights.

3. Fenghuang Ancient Town

While not in Zhangjiajie, many travelers combine trips. Take a long-distance bus from Zhangjiajie Bus Station (4-hour ride).

Practical Tips for Bus Travel

  • Cash Only: Most buses don’t accept digital payments. Carry small bills.
  • Language Barrier: Few drivers speak English. Have your destination written in Chinese (e.g., 武陵源 for Wulingyuan).
  • Avoid Rush Hour: Buses get crowded around 7-9 AM and 5-7 PM.
  • Safety: Keep valuables secure; pickpocketing is rare but possible.

Alternative Transport Options

If buses feel overwhelming, consider:
- Taxi: Affordable for groups (e.g., 100 RMB from downtown to Wulingyuan).
- Bike Rentals: Explore flat areas like the city center.

Final Thoughts

Traveling Zhangjiajie by bus is a rewarding adventure that immerses you in the local culture while saving money. With this guide, you’re ready to conquer the Avatar Mountains like a pro!
