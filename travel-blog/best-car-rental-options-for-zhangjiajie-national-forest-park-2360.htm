Best Car Rental Options for Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is a dream destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and photographers. Famous for its towering sandstone pillars, lush greenery, and breathtaking landscapes (which inspired the floating mountains in Avatar), this UNESCO World Heritage Site attracts millions of visitors every year. While public transportation and guided tours are available, renting a car offers unparalleled flexibility to explore the park and its surrounding areas at your own pace.

Here’s everything you need to know about the best car rental options for your Zhangjiajie adventure.

Why Rent a Car in Zhangjiajie?

Freedom to Explore

Public buses and taxis can be limiting, especially if you want to visit off-the-beaten-path spots like Tianmen Mountain, Baofeng Lake, or the lesser-known valleys of the park. A rental car allows you to create your own itinerary without relying on fixed schedules.

Cost-Effective for Groups

If you’re traveling with family or friends, splitting the cost of a rental car can be cheaper than booking multiple tour packages or hiring private drivers.

Convenience for Photography Enthusiasts

The golden hours (sunrise and sunset) are the best times to capture Zhangjiajie’s surreal landscapes. With a rental car, you can arrive early or stay late without worrying about transportation availability.

Top Car Rental Companies in Zhangjiajie

1. Hertz China

Hertz is a globally recognized brand with a strong presence in China. They offer a variety of vehicles, from compact cars to SUVs, which are ideal for navigating Zhangjiajie’s winding mountain roads.

Pros:
- English-friendly booking platform
- Reliable customer service
- Multiple pickup locations, including Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport

Cons:
- Slightly higher prices compared to local providers

2. Avis China

Avis is another reputable international car rental company operating in Zhangjiajie. They provide well-maintained vehicles and flexible rental plans.

Pros:
- Wide selection of cars
- Optional GPS navigation (helpful for rural areas with poor signal)
- 24/7 roadside assistance

Cons:
- Limited availability during peak tourist seasons

3. Local Providers (e.g., Shenzhou Car Rental, eHi Car Services)

For budget-conscious travelers, local rental companies like Shenzhou and eHi offer competitive rates.

Pros:
- Affordable daily rates
- More flexible rental terms (e.g., hourly rentals)
- Often include basic insurance

Cons:
- Fewer English-speaking staff
- Older vehicle models in some cases

What to Consider Before Renting a Car

1. Driving License Requirements

Foreign visitors must have:
- A valid international driving permit (IDP)
- A passport and Chinese visa
- Some rental companies may require a Chinese-translated version of your license

2. Road Conditions

Zhangjiajie’s roads can be steep and narrow, especially near the park entrances. An SUV or a car with good handling is recommended.

3. Parking Availability

While the park has designated parking areas, they can fill up quickly during peak hours. Arrive early to secure a spot.

4. Fuel Stations

Gas stations are sparse near the park, so plan your refueling stops in advance.

Alternative Transportation Options

If renting a car isn’t for you, consider:
- Private Drivers: Hiring a local driver familiar with the area can be a stress-free alternative.
- Shuttle Buses: The park operates shuttle buses between major attractions.
- Bicycle Rentals: For short distances, cycling can be a fun way to explore nearby villages.

Final Tips for a Smooth Rental Experience

  • Book in advance, especially during holidays like Golden Week.
  • Inspect the car thoroughly before driving off.
  • Download offline maps (Google Maps may not work; use Baidu Maps or Amap instead).
  • Carry cash for tolls and parking fees.

With the right rental car, your Zhangjiajie adventure will be even more memorable. Whether you choose an international brand or a local provider, the freedom to explore this natural wonder on your own terms is unbeatable.
