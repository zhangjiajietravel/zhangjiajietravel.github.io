Zhangjiajie’s Best Playgrounds Near Tourist Spots

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its towering sandstone pillars, lush forests, and breathtaking landscapes. While most visitors come for the natural wonders, families traveling with kids often look for fun, safe, and engaging playgrounds near major attractions. Whether you're taking a break from hiking or need a spot for little ones to burn off energy, these playgrounds near Zhangjiajie’s top tourist spots are perfect for families.

Playgrounds Near Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

1. Avatar Hallelujah Mountain Play Zone

Located just a short walk from the park’s main entrance, this playground is themed around the famous "Avatar" movie, which drew inspiration from Zhangjiajie’s surreal landscape. The play area features climbing structures shaped like floating mountains, slides resembling vine-covered cliffs, and interactive panels about the park’s geology.

Why Kids Love It:
- A mini zip line for adventurous kids.
- Sandpit with hidden "dinosaur fossils" to excavate.
- Shaded seating for parents to relax while keeping an eye on their little explorers.

2. Golden Whip Stream Adventure Playground

After a peaceful stroll along the Golden Whip Stream, kids can unleash their energy at this nature-inspired playground. The design blends seamlessly with the surrounding forest, using wooden structures and rope bridges to mimic the park’s natural beauty.

Highlights:
- A small suspension bridge perfect for young adventurers.
- Monkey-themed climbing frames (watch out—real monkeys sometimes visit!).
- Picnic tables nearby for a quick snack break.

Family-Friendly Play Areas Near Tianzi Mountain

3. Cloud Terrace Play Spot

Tianzi Mountain offers some of the most spectacular views in Zhangjiajie, but the steep trails can be challenging for young children. The Cloud Terrace Play Spot, near the cable car station, provides a safe space for kids to play while adults take in the scenery.

Features:
- A mini rock-climbing wall with safety harnesses.
- Cloud-shaped trampolines for bouncing fun.
- Educational boards about local wildlife.

4. Fairyland Toddler Park

For families with very young kids, this small but well-designed playground near the Tianzi Mountain shuttle stop is ideal. The soft rubber flooring ensures safety, and the whimsical fairy-tale theme keeps toddlers entertained.

Best For:
- Gentle slides and low climbing structures.
- Interactive musical panels.
- Nearby restrooms and baby-changing facilities.

Playgrounds Close to Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon

5. Glass Bridge Adventure Park

The Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge is a thrilling experience, but the surrounding area also has a fantastic playground for kids who aren’t quite ready for the heights. The Adventure Park includes obstacle courses, tunnels, and a mini version of the glass bridge for brave little ones.

Must-Try:
- A rope maze with varying difficulty levels.
- A "spider web" climbing net.
- A viewing deck where parents can watch older kids on the real glass bridge.

6. Canyon Explorer’s Play Zone

Nestled near the canyon’s exit, this playground is perfect for post-hike energy release. The rugged, outdoor-themed play structures let kids pretend they’re explorers discovering hidden treasures.

Kid-Approved Activities:
- A digging area with toy shovels and "gemstones."
- A tunnel slide built into a faux rock formation.
- Plenty of benches in the shade for tired parents.

Play Areas Near Baofeng Lake

7. Lakeside Fairy Tale Garden

Baofeng Lake’s serene waters and boat rides are enchanting, but the nearby fairy tale-themed playground adds extra magic for younger visitors. With structures shaped like castles and boats, it’s a dreamy spot for imaginative play.

What Makes It Special:
- A small splash pad for hot days.
- Storytime corner with benches (occasional puppet shows on weekends).
- Easy access to ice cream vendors.

8. Dragon’s Den Climbing Park

For active kids who love a challenge, this climbing park near Baofeng Lake’s entrance features dragon-themed obstacles, including a "fire-breathing" tunnel (LED lights create the effect).

Thrilling Elements:
- A vertical rope climb with a soft landing.
- A dragon’s tail slide that spirals down.
- A leaderboard for timed challenges (great for competitive kids).

Bonus: Playgrounds in Zhangjiajie City

9. Downtown Zhangjiajie Children’s Park

If you’re staying in the city before or after your park adventures, this large public playground is worth a visit. It’s well-maintained and offers a mix of modern and traditional play equipment.

Why Visit:
- A giant Ferris wheel (smaller version for kids).
- Pedal go-karts for racing fun.
- Street food stalls nearby for quick bites.

10. Night Market Play Corner

Zhangjiajie’s night markets are lively, and this small play area lets kids have fun while parents shop or enjoy local snacks. The evening lighting makes it a unique experience.

Evening Fun Includes:
- Glow-in-the-dark swings.
- A mini carousel.
- Bubble machines that create a magical atmosphere.

Tips for Visiting Playgrounds in Zhangjiajie

  • Timing: Mornings and late afternoons are best to avoid crowds.
  • Weather: Many playgrounds are uncovered, so bring hats and sunscreen.
  • Hydration: Carry water bottles—vendors aren’t always nearby.
  • Safety: Always supervise kids, especially near natural attractions where wildlife may be present.

Zhangjiajie isn’t just for hikers and photographers—it’s also a fantastic destination for families. These playgrounds near major tourist spots ensure that kids have just as much fun as adults while exploring this natural wonderland.
