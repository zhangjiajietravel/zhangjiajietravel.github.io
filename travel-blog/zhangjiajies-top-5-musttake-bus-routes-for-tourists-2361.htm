Zhangjiajie’s Top 5 Must-Take Bus Routes for Tourists

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the inspiration for the floating mountains in Avatar, is a dream destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. While the towering sandstone pillars and lush forests steal the spotlight, navigating this vast national park can be daunting. Fortunately, Zhangjiajie’s efficient bus system connects major attractions, making it easier for tourists to explore. Here are the top five must-take bus routes that will help you maximize your visit.

1. Zhangjiajie City to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (Wulingyuan Entrance)

Why Take This Route?

This is the most popular bus route for first-time visitors, as it leads directly to the park’s main entrance at Wulingyuan. The journey takes about 40 minutes and offers glimpses of rural Hunan’s stunning landscapes.

Key Stops & Highlights

  • Zhangjiajie Central Bus Station: The starting point, where you can purchase park tickets and catch the bus.
  • Wulingyuan Bus Terminal: From here, it’s a short walk to the park entrance.
  • Nearby Attractions: The Bailong Elevator and Yuanjiajie Scenic Area (the Avatar Hallelujah Mountain) are easily accessible.

Pro Tip

Buses run frequently (every 10–15 minutes), so there’s no need to rush. Arrive early to avoid crowds at the park entrance.

2. Wulingyuan to Tianzi Mountain Cable Car Station

Why Take This Route?

Tianzi Mountain offers some of Zhangjiajie’s most breathtaking panoramic views. This bus route takes you to the cable car station, where you can ascend to the summit without a strenuous hike.

Key Stops & Highlights

  • Tianzi Mountain Bus Stop: The final stop before the cable car.
  • Heavenly Platform: A viewpoint with unobstructed vistas of the sea of peaks.
  • Imperial Brush Peak: A iconic rock formation resembling a giant brush.

Pro Tip

If you prefer hiking, skip the cable car and take the bus to the trailhead at Tianzi Mountain’s base. The climb is challenging but rewarding.

3. Zhangjiajie National Forest Park to Golden Whip Stream

Why Take This Route?

Golden Whip Stream is a serene, flat walking trail perfect for families and those seeking a leisurely stroll amidst nature. The bus drops you near the trailhead, eliminating the need for long walks from the park entrance.

Key Stops & Highlights

  • Golden Whip Stream Entrance: Start of the 7.5-kilometer trail.
  • Monkey Encounters: Wild macaques often greet visitors (keep snacks hidden!).
  • Natural Landmarks: Look for the "Golden Whip Rock" and "Reunion Rock."

Pro Tip

Wear comfortable shoes and bring water—there are few vendors along the trail.

4. Zhangjiajie to Glass Bridge at Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon

Why Take This Route?

The Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge is one of the world’s highest and longest glass-bottom bridges. The bus ride from Zhangjiajie city takes about 1.5 hours but is worth the thrill.

Key Stops & Highlights

  • Grand Canyon Parking Lot: The bus drops you here; a short shuttle takes you to the bridge.
  • The Glass Bridge: A 430-meter-long, 300-meter-high adrenaline rush.
  • Grand Canyon Hike: After crossing, explore the canyon’s waterfalls and caves.

Pro Tip

Book bridge tickets in advance—it’s a hugely popular attraction with limited daily entries.

5. Zhangjiajie to Fenghuang Ancient Town (Optional Day Trip)

Why Take This Route?

While not within Zhangjiajie, Fenghuang (Phoenix Ancient Town) is a magical detour. The 4-hour bus ride transports you to a well-preserved Ming-Qing dynasty town with stilted houses and lantern-lit streets.

Key Stops & Highlights

  • Fenghuang Bus Station: Arrival point near the old town.
  • Tuojia Stilt Houses: Traditional architecture over the Tuo River.
  • Nightlife: The town glows with reflections on the water after dark.

Pro Tip

Stay overnight to experience Fenghuang’s enchanting evening atmosphere.

Bonus: Navigating Zhangjiajie’s Bus System

  • Payment: Most buses accept cash (small bills) or WeChat Pay.
  • Frequency: Buses run from 6:30 AM to 7:30 PM; check schedules for last departures.
  • Language Barrier: Have your destination written in Chinese or use a translation app.

Exploring Zhangjiajie by bus is not only budget-friendly but also lets you soak in the region’s beauty beyond the famous peaks. Whether you’re chasing Avatar vistas or hidden trails, these routes ensure a seamless adventure.
