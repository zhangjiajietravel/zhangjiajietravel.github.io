Zhangjiajie by Train: The Ultimate Travel Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhangjiajie, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the inspiration for the floating mountains in Avatar, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. While many travelers opt for flights, taking the train to Zhangjiajie offers a scenic, budget-friendly, and immersive way to experience China’s breathtaking landscapes. This guide covers everything you need to know about traveling to Zhangjiajie by train, including routes, ticket booking, must-see attractions, and insider tips.

Why Take the Train to Zhangjiajie?

Scenic Journeys Through China’s Heartland

Train travel in China isn’t just about convenience—it’s an experience. Routes to Zhangjiajie pass through rolling hills, terraced fields, and dramatic karst formations, especially if coming from Guilin or Chongqing. The slower pace allows you to soak in views you’d miss on a flight.

Cost-Effective Travel

Compared to flights, trains are often cheaper, especially for last-minute bookings. Overnight sleeper trains save on accommodation costs, making them ideal for budget travelers.

Eco-Friendly Option

Trains have a lower carbon footprint than planes, aligning with sustainable travel trends.

Popular Train Routes to Zhangjiajie

From Beijing (12–15 Hours)

High-speed G-trains (6–7 hours to Changsha, then transfer) or direct overnight sleepers (K/T-trains) offer flexibility. The latter is great for maximizing daylight sightseeing.

From Shanghai (8–12 Hours)

High-speed trains via Changsha take 8–9 hours, while slower trains (K/Z-series) run overnight.

From Guangzhou (6–8 Hours)

Multiple high-speed D/G-trains connect daily, with the fastest taking just under 6 hours.

From Chongqing (5–6 Hours)

A scenic route through Wuling Mountains, with frequent high-speed D-trains.

Booking Train Tickets

Online Platforms

Use 12306.cn (official site, English available) or third-party apps like Trip.com for easy booking.

Ticket Types

  • Second Class: Affordable but busy.
  • First Class: More legroom.
  • Business Class: Luxurious, with meals.
  • Sleeper Berths: Soft sleepers (4 beds/cabin) are ideal for overnight trips.

Pro Tips

  • Book early, especially during holidays (Golden Week, Spring Festival).
  • Download your e-ticket or pick up a paper copy at the station.

Arriving in Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie Railway Station vs. Zhangjiajie West Station

Most trains stop at Zhangjiajie Railway Station, closer to downtown. High-speed trains may terminate at Zhangjiajie West Station, 12 km from the city—take a taxi or bus (Route 4/16) to the center.

Getting to the National Park

From downtown, buses (Route 10/12) or taxis (¥30–50) go to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park’s main gates in 30–40 minutes.

Must-See Attractions in Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

  • Yuanjiajie: The "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" area.
  • Tianzi Mountain: Panoramic views of peaks shrouded in mist.
  • Golden Whip Stream: An easy hike along a crystal-clear river.

Tianmen Mountain

  • Tianmen Cave: The "Heaven’s Gate" hole in the mountain.
  • Glass Skywalk: Not for the faint-hearted!
  • Cable Car: One of the world’s longest, with dizzying drops.

Grand Canyon & Glass Bridge

Walk the world’s highest glass-bottomed bridge (430 meters long) for adrenaline-pumping views.

Where to Stay

Near the National Park

  • Wulingyuan District: Hotels like Pullman Zhangjiajie offer easy park access.
  • Hostels: Budget options like Zhangjiajie Qixiang Hostel cater to backpackers.

Downtown Zhangjiajie

  • Luxury: Sunshine Hotel (near the train station).
  • Mid-range: Holiday Inn Express.

Food & Local Delicacies

Don’t miss:
- Tujia Bacon: Smoky, cured pork.
- Sanxiaguo: A spicy hotpot with local herbs.
- Sour Fish Soup: A refreshing Miao minority dish.

Insider Tips for Train Travel

  • Pack Snacks: Train food is basic; bring instant noodles or fruit.
  • Wi-Fi: Limited on trains—download offline maps/entertainment.
  • Language: Learn basic Mandarin phrases or use translation apps.
  • Altitude: Some areas are 1,000+ meters high; hydrate and rest.

Seasonal Considerations

  • Spring (April–June): Lush greenery, fewer crowds.
  • Summer (July–August): Busy but great for clear skies.
  • Autumn (September–November): Cool weather, golden foliage.
  • Winter (December–February): Snow-capped peaks, but some trails close.

Day Trips from Zhangjiajie

  • Fenghuang Ancient Town: A 3-hour train ride to this photogenic riverside village.
  • Wulingyuan: Explore nearby caves and minority villages.

Final Logistics

  • Visa: Ensure your China visa is valid.
  • Cash: Small vendors may not accept cards.
  • Comfort: Wear layers—mountain weather changes fast.

With its surreal landscapes and seamless train connections, Zhangjiajie is a destination that rewards the journey as much as the destination. All aboard for adventure!
